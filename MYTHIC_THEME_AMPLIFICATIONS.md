# Mythic × Theme Amplifications

Status: In review — resonant amplifications and dissonant redemption/fall arcs.

## Overview

Mythic Paths and Themes both shape who a character *is* — but they operate at different scales. Themes are lived identity (who you were before adventuring, how that forms you). Mythic Paths are cosmic destiny (what you are becoming, how the multiverse marks you). When they align — when a character's Theme and Path share thematic resonance — they *amplify* each other. When they clash, they create the richest narrative territory in the game: redemption arcs, fall-from-grace tragedies, and the slow transformation of someone forced to reconcile who they were with what they are becoming.

## Locked-in design decisions

From earlier: **Mythic interaction is amplifying with dissonance arcs.** Resonant combos gain bonuses. Dissonant combos unlock narrative paths with unique abilities for playing against type. The AI DM leans into dissonance for roleplay opportunities.

This document operationalizes that.

## Scope note

There are **12 player-accessible Mythic Paths** × **21 Themes** = 252 possible combinations. This document does NOT exhaustively catalog all of them. Instead, it focuses on:

1. **~15 strongly resonant combos** — amplifications granting mechanical bonuses
2. **~7 dissonant combos** — opening Redemption Arc or Fall From Grace narrative paths
3. **The Redemption and Corrupted Dawn paths themselves** — explained as inherently arc-driven

Combinations not listed here function normally — both Path and Theme work independently, and the AI DM can invoke generative narrative moments based on shared tags when appropriate.

## How amplifications work

**Resonant combos** activate automatically when a character has both the listed Theme and Path. They provide a small, specific mechanical bonus that reflects the thematic alignment. The bonus scales with the character's Mythic Tier:
- T1 (L5): Minor bonus
- T2 (L10): Moderate bonus  
- T3 (L15): Significant bonus
- T4 (L20): Major bonus (capstone)

**Dissonant combos** are activated differently — they do not grant automatic bonuses. Instead, they **unlock a narrative arc** that plays out over multiple sessions. The AI DM tracks the character's progress along the arc, delivering specific story beats and gradually revealing unique mechanical abilities as the character moves toward reconciliation, redemption, or full fall.

## Resonant Amplifications

### Hierophant + Acolyte — "Saintly Vessel"
- **Shared identity:** Faith as foundation. Both paths assume the character's relationship with the divine is central to who they are.
- **T1 bonus:** Your Mythic Power pool expands by 1. Your Acolyte's Spiritual Counsel (L5) can now be performed during a short rest (instead of consuming the once-per-long-rest limit) on a creature who has received your Hierophant blessing.
- **T2 bonus:** Your Faithkeeper blessing (Acolyte L11) lasts 2 hours instead of 1, and recipients gain +1 Hierophant Surge die on attack rolls (d4 at T2).
- **T3 bonus:** Your Divine Intercessor (Acolyte L17) Heal option also restores one Mythic Power to each ally within 30 feet who is also Mythic. Your faith *gives* power.
- **T4 bonus:** You are treated as a legitimate cleric of your chosen deity regardless of class. Your deity's personal attention is on you — the AI DM may deliver direct signs, visions, or minor miracles during significant moments.

### Angel + Knight of the Order — "Celestial Chevalier"
- **Shared identity:** Sworn service made cosmic. The Knight's oath ascends to the level of divine contract.
- **T1 bonus:** Your Oath Unbroken (Knight L5) save advantage extends to divine damage types (necrotic, fire from fiendish sources).
- **T2 bonus:** When you use your Banner of the Order (L11), your wings manifest briefly and an ally within 60 feet can be granted flight at their walking speed for the duration.
- **T3 bonus:** Your Paragon of the Order aura (L17) is empowered — the +1 AC and attack bonus to allies now comes with **resistance to radiant and necrotic damage** (both) for its duration.
- **T4 bonus:** Once per long rest, you can summon a celestial herald (CR equivalent to 1/4 your character level) to fight alongside you for 1 hour. This is above and beyond your Mythic Power pool.

### Azata + Entertainer — "The Muse-Touched"
- **Shared identity:** Wild beauty, creative chaos, freedom. The Azata finds the Entertainer's art to be a form of cosmic poetry.
- **T1 bonus:** Your Read the Room (Entertainer L5) also reveals the dominant emotional *longing* of the crowd — what they wish for that they cannot name.
- **T2 bonus:** Your Showstopper (L11) charm effect affects fey creatures normally (they are not immune), and your charmed targets gain a temporary joy — they are *genuinely* captivated by your art, not just magically.
- **T3 bonus:** Legend of the Stage's performance options (Anthem of Valor, Dirge of Sorrow, Ballad of Mending) now have a 120-foot radius instead of 60 feet. The fey winds carry your song further.
- **T4 bonus:** Once per long rest, you can declare a performance that transcends mortal limits — the AI DM narrates a moment where your art literally changes reality (a tyrant's heart melts, a curse breaks, a hidden path reveals itself). The specific effect is DM discretion but always significant.

### Gold Dragon + Noble — "Dragon-Blooded Sovereign"
- **Shared identity:** Legitimate authority made divine. The Noble's birthright and the Gold Dragon's honor create an archetype of righteous rule.
- **T1 bonus:** Your Weight of Name (Noble L5) now grants advantage on *all* social checks in noble courts, not just political ones. Dragons recognize a peer.
- **T2 bonus:** Your Noblesse Oblige commands (L11) can be given to creatures of any intelligence (not just commoners/low-ranking). Your draconic bearing makes even the proud listen.
- **T3 bonus:** Sovereign Presence (L17) deference save becomes Charisma (not just Wisdom) — creatures who fail can never quite shake the sense that you are more important than the political reality suggests.
- **T4 bonus:** Once per campaign arc, your political favor now includes the personal intervention of a metallic dragon (or other lawful good power) — armies march, treaties are brokered, entire kingdoms shift allegiance.

### Lich + Haunted One — "The Vessel of Grief"
- **Shared identity:** Darkness survived becomes darkness wielded. The Haunted One's trauma is the foundation stone of the Lich's ascension.
- **T1 bonus:** Your Mark of Dread (Haunted One L5) channel dread's DC is increased by +2 against creatures of the undead type. The Lich's presence amplifies your already-considerable terror.
- **T2 bonus:** Your Inured to Horror (L11) fear immunity extends — you are also immune to being charmed or compelled by creatures of undead, fiend, or aberration type. Your patron lich recognizes a kindred spirit.
- **T3 bonus:** Your Gaze into the Abyss (L17) Confrontation ability now affects undead at full power — even high-tier undead struggle against your will. Banishment and Break options impose disadvantage on the creature's resistances for their duration.
- **T4 bonus:** Your Aura of Unease affects **all** supernatural creatures (including celestials, fey, and elementals) — not just undead, fiends, and aberrations. The darkness you carry is noticed by everything that is not mortal.

### Trickster + Charlatan — "The Great Deceiver"
- **Shared identity:** The art of the lie at cosmic scale. The Charlatan's mortal deception is amplified by the Trickster's chaos magic.
- **T1 bonus:** Your Second Skin disguises (Charlatan L5) gain a subtle magical dimension — they resist Detect Magic detection as if they were only mundane disguises, *even though they have a faint magical quality*. They pass any magical scrutiny below 6th level.
- **T2 bonus:** Your Web of Lies (L11) maintained identities now number up to 5 (instead of 3). The Trickster's chaos helps you remember them all perfectly.
- **T3 bonus:** Your Perfect Con's flawless identity (L17) duration extends to 24 hours (instead of 8). Your identity doesn't unravel; it settles deeper.
- **T4 bonus:** Once per long rest, you can enter a state where **no lie you tell can be detected as false** for 1 hour. This includes magical detection (Zone of Truth, etc.), divine sight (a paladin's Divine Sense won't see through you), and truth magic of all kinds. The AI DM should note: this is one of the most powerful social capstones in the system.

### Aeon + Sage — "The Pattern-Keeper"
- **Shared identity:** Knowledge as cosmic order. The Sage studies the patterns; the Aeon upholds them.
- **T1 bonus:** Your Studied Mind's recall ability (Sage L5) now reveals **two** pieces of information per use (instead of one). The Aeon's connection reveals deeper truths.
- **T2 bonus:** Your Analytical Eye (L11) also reveals a creature's *role in the cosmic pattern* — whether it is fulfilling its destined purpose, or acting against it. This is valuable information the DM can provide.
- **T3 bonus:** Living Library's deep study (L17) now takes only 1 minute (instead of 10 minutes), and reveals connections to past events the character may not have personally witnessed.
- **T4 bonus:** You gain a form of **foresight** — once per long rest, the AI DM reveals one piece of information about an imminent future event within the next 24 hours. This is not divination magic; it is pattern recognition at cosmic scale.

### Demon + Mercenary Veteran — "The Crimson Contract"
- **Shared identity:** Blood and rage made profitable. The Mercenary's pragmatism meets the Demon's chaos.
- **T1 bonus:** Your Killing Floor (Mercenary L11 Synergy trigger, see Party Synergies) activates even when solo — you gain +2 to attack and damage while below half HP. The Demon's rage fuels you.
- **T2 bonus:** Your Battlefield Opportunist's tactical withdrawal (L11) now also allows you to deal an additional 1d6 damage per ally you bring with you to safety. You profit from every life saved.
- **T3 bonus:** Your Dead Eye (L17) critical strike triggers Demon Surge — on a hit, you regain 1 Mythic Power. The violence feeds your power.
- **T4 bonus:** Once per long rest, you can declare a **Blood Contract** — choose a target within 60 feet. For the next 1 minute, every attack you make against them deals critical hit damage, and you regain HP equal to half the damage dealt. The target must be a creature the Mercenary has been "hired" to kill (narratively, contract can be informal — revenge, protection of allies, etc.).

### Devil + Noble — "The Pact-Bound Heir"
- **Shared identity:** Order through binding agreement. Both paths build power through contracts — Devil's infernal bargains, Noble's political oaths.
- **T1 bonus:** Your Weight of Name (Noble L5) audiences extend to infernal nobility — you can request an audience with a minor devil or fiend equivalent (via ritual), and they are obligated to hear you out under infernal law.
- **T2 bonus:** Your Noblesse Oblige commands (L11) are now magically reinforced — targets who fail the save feel genuinely compelled for a brief moment, not just socially obligated. Additionally, your noble letters of introduction (L11) function as minor bargaining chits among infernal creatures.
- **T3 bonus:** Sovereign Presence (L17) extends to fiends — they treat you as a minor infernal noble. You can safely negotiate in devilish courts if you walk carefully.
- **T4 bonus:** Once per campaign arc, your political favor can be traded for an **infernal favor** — you call in a debt from a major devil or infernal faction. The scope is significant (a legion of lesser devils, a soul-binding, an infernal artifact) but the cost is proportional. The AI DM should make this a dramatic negotiation.

### Legend + Any Theme of Mastery — "The Archetype Made Real"
- **Shared identity:** Legend is the path of mortal mastery at cosmic scale. It amplifies whatever the character already is, making their Theme feel legendary.
- **T1 bonus:** The Theme's L5 ability's cooldown is reduced by one step (once per long → once per short; once per short → once per encounter; per encounter → at will).
- **T2 bonus:** The Theme's L11 ability's Expertise Die increases by one size (d4 → d6).
- **T3 bonus:** The Theme's L17 capstone's cooldown is reduced by one step (once per campaign arc → once per long rest; once per long rest → once per short rest).
- **T4 bonus:** The character is recognized as a **legendary version of their Theme archetype** — other characters of the same Theme (NPCs, companions) treat them as teacher, peer, or aspirational figure. This is the only path where the mechanical bonus is intentionally Theme-agnostic; Legend amplifies *whatever you already are*.

### Redemption + [Former Dark Theme] — "The Atonement Arc"
- **Shared identity:** A character who fell and is rising. This Path is *specifically* for characters whose Theme progression took them to a dark place (Knight's Fallen Path, Fallen Aasimar's Embraced Shadow, etc.)
- **Mechanic:** Redemption Path's abilities scale based on acts of atonement. The AI DM tracks meaningful deeds — saving innocents, refusing temptation, returning what was stolen, bearing witness to atrocity and speaking truth about it. Each atonement act grants an increment toward the next mechanical threshold.
- **T1 bonus:** Once a minimum of 3 atonement acts have been performed, your Mythic Power pool grows by 1. You begin to feel the path forward.
- **T2 bonus:** Once 10 atonement acts: your previous Theme abilities that were lost due to fall are restored at 50% capacity (reduced uses, reduced duration, etc.).
- **T3 bonus:** Once 20 atonement acts: your previous Theme abilities are fully restored. Additionally, you gain a unique ability — **Witness** — once per long rest, when an ally is about to commit an act you once would have committed, you can grant them a moment of clarity. They see the full consequences of the act as you would have seen them, and may choose to refuse (they are not forced to refuse — they are *informed*).
- **T4 bonus:** Once 40 atonement acts: your redemption is complete. The AI DM narrates a significant moment where the cosmos acknowledges your return. Your Theme abilities are now *better* than they were — each gains a small additional effect (DM determines specifics, but the tier is comparable to an L17 Theme upgrade). Your scars become your strength.

### Corrupted Dawn + [Fallen Character] — "The Consequence Made Manifest"
- **Shared identity:** The opposite of Redemption. This Path is *specifically* for characters who have embraced corruption — Knight Fallen Path, Fallen Aasimar Embraced Shadow, Scourge Aasimar who compound zealotry into atrocity, etc.
- **Mechanic:** Corrupted Dawn's abilities scale with darker acts — acts of calculated cruelty, betrayal, acceptance of evil as a tool. The AI DM tracks these as the inverse of atonement acts.
- **T1 bonus:** Minimum 3 corrupt acts: your Mythic Power pool grows by 2 (instead of 1 — the dark path is more generous, and more demanding). You gain disadvantage on Persuasion checks with good-aligned creatures who know of your deeds.
- **T2 bonus:** 10 corrupt acts: your Theme's dark abilities (if any — e.g., Haunted One's necrotic channel, Fallen Aasimar's Shadow Step, etc.) gain enhanced effects. Your Intimidation becomes almost supernatural.
- **T3 bonus:** 20 corrupt acts: you gain a **Dark Thrall** — a loyal NPC servant (minor demon, undead, or mortal corrupted by you) who serves for life. The AI DM determines who, but they are genuinely devoted to you. However, you also acquire a **hunter** — a righteous force (paladin order, celestial agent, vengeful family) now actively pursues you.
- **T4 bonus:** 40 corrupt acts: your transformation is complete. You gain significant dark abilities (the scope depends on your specific Theme and accumulated corruption). You are also no longer welcome in most civilized lands. The capstone of Corrupted Dawn is power + isolation. *"You chose this. It was not cheap."*

## Dissonant Combos (Redemption Arcs)

These combinations do not grant automatic bonuses. Instead, they unlock a **narrative arc** with gradual mechanical rewards tied to character development.

### Angel + Criminal — "The Redeemed Thief"
- **Dissonance:** The Angel's pact is pure service to a greater cause; the Criminal's life has been one of betrayal and self-interest. Yet — sometimes the Angel chooses the former criminal specifically *because* they know what they are leaving behind.
- **Arc:** The character starts with reduced Angel benefits (their Mythic Power pool is 1 smaller, their Surge dice are one size smaller). As they perform **9 Redemption Acts** (major moments of refusing their former ways, protecting innocents, returning what was stolen), the mechanics gradually restore.
- **Unique arc ability unlocked at full restoration:** "**The Thief's Sacrifice**" — once per long rest, you can steal a divine attack or effect targeting an ally within 60 feet and take it yourself. Any damage it would deal to the ally is instead dealt to you; any beneficial effect that was redirected (a curse, etc.) is now on you. The Criminal's old skill of redirecting attention is weaponized in the service of others.

### Demon + Folk Hero — "Rage With Purpose"
- **Dissonance:** The Demon is pure chaos, hunger, violence for its own sake. The Folk Hero stands FOR something — community, decency, the small folk. These should be incompatible.
- **Arc:** The character can invoke Demon rage, but each use of Demon-granted abilities automatically damages one friendly creature within 10 feet (DM's choice). The rage hungers. As the character builds **7 Community Anchors** (meaningful moments of using the rage to protect rather than merely destroy — saving villagers, stopping a raid, intimidating tyrants instead of butchering them), the rage learns restraint.
- **Unique arc ability unlocked:** "**Righteous Chaos**" — your Demon rage can now be channeled specifically toward injustice. While raging, you deal +1d6 damage per tier of Mythic Path against creatures the common folk would call oppressors (tyrants, slavers, corrupt officials). Against non-oppressor targets, your damage bonus is normal. The rage now chooses its victims.

### Lich + Acolyte — "The Grieving Priest"
- **Dissonance:** The Acolyte's faith is in life, renewal, the cycle. The Lich's power is in denial of death. The Acolyte who takes Lich Path has likely lost someone beyond what they could accept.
- **Arc:** The character can access Lich abilities, but each use costs them spiritually — they lose one point of their deity's Piety for each use of Lich-specific power (if using the Piety system). As they build **9 Moments of Acceptance** (allowing something to die that they could have prevented, releasing a soul, attending a funeral and truly mourning), the cost diminishes.
- **Unique arc ability unlocked:** "**The Vessel That Remembers**" — once per long rest, you can commune with any soul that died within the last year whom you have personally met in life. They answer one question truthfully. Your Lich magic has become a bridge rather than a cage for the dead. Your deity may or may not approve; the AI DM tracks this.

### Trickster + Knight of the Order — "The Masked Oath"
- **Dissonance:** The Knight's oath is absolute; the Trickster's power is in breaking rules. A Knight who takes Trickster Path either has a secret that breaks their oath, OR has realized their oath requires deception to fulfill.
- **Arc:** The character cannot use their Oath Unbroken's social benefits while using Trickster abilities in the same session. The two identities cannot publicly coexist. As they perform **7 acts of Secret Service** (missions that uphold their oath's spirit while breaking its letter), the tension crystallizes into synthesis.
- **Unique arc ability unlocked:** "**The Hidden Knight**" — you can create a false identity that the magic of your order recognizes as legitimate. Your order accepts the false you as one of them — you can invoke your order's resources through the false identity (lodging, ceremony, ritual) without the order realizing two of you exist. This is an advanced application of your oath combined with Trickster deception.

### Devil + Acolyte — "The Tempted Priest"
- **Dissonance:** The Acolyte's faith is in a divine patron; the Devil's pact is literally opposite authority. The Acolyte who takes Devil Path is either being tempted, OR has made a desperate bargain in service of their actual faith.
- **Arc:** The character gains Devil abilities but cannot use Acolyte abilities in the same long rest period — the two patrons reject each other's channel. As they perform **7 Acts of Clarified Faith** (using Devil power specifically for purposes aligned with Acolyte faith — a deal with a devil to save an innocent, a contract that traps a greater evil), they begin to harmonize.
- **Unique arc ability unlocked:** "**The Sacred Contract**" — once per long rest, you can make a formal bargain with a creature that is magically binding on both parties. This is not simply an Infernal contract — it carries divine weight too. Breaking the agreement invokes both your patron's displeasure *and* your deity's reaction. The AI DM should note: this ability makes you dangerous to cross.

### Gold Dragon + Urchin — "The Forgotten Heir"
- **Dissonance:** The Gold Dragon is the paragon of righteous authority; the Urchin has been overlooked, unnamed, invisible. For both to coexist is to discover that the Urchin was *always* something more than they seemed.
- **Arc:** The AI DM reveals, over multiple sessions, hints that the Urchin has significant lineage they never knew — royal bloodline, draconic heritage, divinely-marked birth, etc. As the character investigates and claims pieces of this identity (visiting ancestral sites, being recognized by those who knew the lineage, accepting or refusing the inheritance), the Gold Dragon path's benefits emerge.
- **Unique arc ability unlocked:** "**The Claim**" — once per campaign arc, the character can make a formal claim to a legacy (a throne, a territory, a sacred title). The AI DM adjudicates whether it succeeds, but the *possibility* is always there. Common folk who learn of the Urchin's true lineage respond with either awe or betrayal — the character has to navigate what becoming someone of importance means to the people who loved them when they were no one.

### Angel + Mercenary Veteran — "The Contract Fulfilled"
- **Dissonance:** The Angel's pact is eternal service; the Mercenary's contracts are limited, transactional. The Mercenary who takes Angel Path has made a contract with the universe itself — and it came due.
- **Arc:** The character must periodically **honor** the Angel's pact through acts of selfless service — the Mercenary cannot charge for these. If they refuse 3 pact-requested services in a row, they lose Angel access until atonement. As they perform **7 Pact Services** without complaint (or with internal complaint but external compliance), the mercenary's identity shifts.
- **Unique arc ability unlocked:** "**The Mercenary's Gift**" — once per long rest, you can bestow a pact service on another creature. They gain the benefits of an angelic blessing for 1 hour — Angel Surge dice, limited flight, resistance to chosen damage type. The cost is paid by you, and you cannot gift this to the same creature more than once per week. The Mercenary's contract is now a gift she gives.

## Special: The Two Shadow Paths

### Redemption Path — How it works
As described above, this path is inherently arc-based rather than amplification-based. It is the one Mythic Path where *every* Theme combination creates a unique story. Characters who took their Theme down a dark path (Fallen Knight, Embraced Shadow Fallen Aasimar, Scourge who compounded zealotry, even a Haunted One who lost themselves to their trauma) can take Redemption Path and have their abilities scale with genuine narrative atonement.

The AI DM's responsibility: track atonement acts honestly. Not every kind act is atonement. Redemption requires sacrifice — the character has to give up something, not just do something nice. The AI DM should call out true atonement when it happens and withhold the mechanical benefit if the player is trying to speedrun "kindness moments" without actually changing.

### Corrupted Dawn Path — How it works
The dark mirror of Redemption. Characters who have embraced corruption can take Corrupted Dawn, and their abilities scale with *further* corruption — acts of calculated cruelty, exploitation of power, betrayal of those who trusted them. The AI DM tracks these.

The AI DM's responsibility: honor the player's choice without judgment. This is not a trick or a secret "really you should redeem yourself" path — the player chose this. The mechanics reflect the full power and full cost of that choice. The character becomes powerful, feared, and isolated. Good-aligned NPCs may hunt them; fiends and corrupted powers may court them. The AI DM should make the world respond to the corruption — not just narratively flinch from it, but actually change in response.

## Summary & Design Notes

**Total amplifications designed:** 11 resonant combos + 7 dissonant arc combos + 2 special Shadow Paths (Redemption, Corrupted Dawn) = 20 documented interactions.

**What's NOT covered:** Most Path × Theme combinations do not have hardcoded interactions. A Hierophant + Mercenary Veteran is a perfectly valid character concept that doesn't have a unique amplification — the Hierophant still works, the Mercenary still works, and the AI DM can narrate moments where they resonate or clash at appropriate moments.

**Design philosophy:**
- **Resonant combos feel "right"** — the player intuitively understands that these two belong together, and the mechanical bonus validates that intuition.
- **Dissonant combos feel "dangerous"** — the player intuits that these combinations will create narrative friction, and the mechanical cost/benefit structure makes that friction meaningful rather than punishing.
- **The shadow paths (Redemption, Corrupted Dawn) feel "transformational"** — the character doesn't stay who they were; they become something new, with the AI DM tracking the journey.

**Integration with other systems:**
- Party Synergies (PARTY_SYNERGIES.md) may trigger additional effects with specific Path characters in the party
- Subclass × Theme Synergies (SUBCLASS_THEME_SYNERGIES.md) stack with Mythic × Theme amplifications — a Battle Master Fighter + Soldier + Angel Path character benefits from ALL THREE synergy layers
- The Prelude Session (for characters who have one) can plant seeds for these interactions — a character whose prelude establishes them as devoutly religious is narratively primed for Hierophant/Angel paths

**Next phases:**
1. Review for balance outliers — some amplifications (especially T4 bonuses) may need scaling
2. Implementation planning — database schema for tracking amplification state, AI DM prompt integration, narrative arc tracking
